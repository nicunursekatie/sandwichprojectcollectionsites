As we enter the season of giving, I want to share something that reflects the heart of this community. Attached is our 2025 one-page annual report, a snapshot of the impact you helped create this year.
Over the past five years, The Sandwich Project has grown from a small, grassroots volunteer effort into a vital part of community food support across Metro Atlanta. This year, to ensure long-term sustainability and strengthen our ability to respond to rising need, we hired our first Executive Director. This milestone reflects your trust, your partnership, and the steady growth of a volunteer-powered movement that continues to make an incredible difference.

Here are a few highlights from this year:

450,000 sandwiches made so far in 2025
We are on track to reach nearly 500,000 by year-end. Volunteers of all ages made these sandwiches one at a time, and they were delivered weekly to more than 70 partner organizations across Metro Atlanta.
More than 2.2 million sandwiches delivered since 2020
What began with just 317 sandwiches in April 2020 is now a consistent, reliable distribution network reaching neighbors across high-need zip codes every week.

Responding to rising need with timely, efficient action
Throughout 2025, disruptions to food assistance and rising grocery costs increased demand from families and organizations we serve. Our team and volunteers responded quickly and efficiently to meet urgent needs. For example, during periods of extreme cold, we coordinated special call-outs for blankets and warm layers, ensuring neighbors stayed safe while still receiving food support.

Partnerships that reach vulnerable neighbors
This year, we strengthened our partnership with Solidarity Sandy Springs and Giving Grace to support families who face real barriers accessing food. For example, homebound households received grocery boxes and fresh sandwiches delivered directly to them. This is only one example of how we reach vulnerable neighbors throughout Metro Atlanta.
Expanding to the University of Georgia
A group of former TSP high school interns launched a student-led chapter at UGA, mobilizing college students to address food insecurity both in Athens-Clarke County and among their peers. Their leadership is growing our mission beyond Atlanta.
