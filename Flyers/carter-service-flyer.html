From Plains, Georgia to Your Hands:
Service That Matters

Jimmy Carter believed you don't need to be powerful to make a difference—you just need to show up and do the work. That's what The Sandwich Project is all about.

What We Do:

Since 2020, volunteers across Atlanta have made 2.2+ million sandwiches to fight food insecurity. We don't ask people to apply or wait in line—we just show up where they are and offer food with dignity.

Why Sandwiches?

  • Fast impact—help reaches people right away
  • Anyone can help—just like Habitat for Humanity, but with food
  • Reaches people who might not know about other resources

What You'll Do on May 7th:
Your class will make sandwiches that'll be delivered to neighbors experiencing homelessness within 48 hours. It's simple, but it's real—and it adds up. One class can make 200+ meals in an hour.

"Small Acts, Big Impact"

Jimmy Carter spent decades building homes with Habitat for Humanity—not because it solved the housing crisis, but because showing up matters. Making sandwiches is the same kind of service: tangible, accessible, and rooted in the belief that everyone deserves dignity.

Want to Keep Going?

After May 7th, you can volunteer with your family, start a collection at your place of worship, or follow our work online.
